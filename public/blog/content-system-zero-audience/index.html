A simple weekly content system for founders with zero audience

Zero followers feels like shouting into an empty room. So don't shout. When you have no audience, distribution isn't broadcasting — it's borrowing other people's audiences, going where your buyers already gather, and doing things that don't scale.

The mistake nearly every first-time founder makes: they post to their own empty profile and wait. With zero followers, that's a feedback loop with no feedback. The reframe that changes everything is simple — you don't have an audience yet, so go borrow one.

The weekly loop

One repeatable week, run on a loop until something clicks:

  1. Make one core piece — a short post, thread, or teardown that genuinely helps your ideal customer. Use a fixed set of angles so you never start from blank.
  2. Place it where the audience already is — relevant subreddits, Indie Hackers, niche Discords/Slacks, LinkedIn groups. Give value first; mention your thing lightly, if at all.
  3. Have 10 real conversations — DM or reply to 10 ideal customers. Ask about their problem, don't pitch. This is the highest-signal thing you can do at zero scale.
  4. Drop one compounding asset — a free tool, a template, or an SEO post that keeps working after you stop promoting it.

Borrowed audiences beat owned audiences (at first)

You don't need followers to post in a community of 200,000 people who have your exact problem. Communities, launch platforms (Product Hunt, BetaList), other people's newsletters, and search engines all let you reach an audience you didn't have to build. Do this for a few months and — quietly — you'll have built one of your own.

Do things that don't scale. Fifty real conversations validate an idea better than fifty thousand cold impressions. The founder who can win the first 50 users by hand earns the right to automate the next 5,000.

Plant compounding assets early

Posts disappear in a day. Two things don't: search and tools. A helpful article ranks and brings readers for years. A free tool gets shared and bookmarked. Both work while you sleep and neither needs an existing audience — which is exactly why they matter most when you're starting from zero.

Measure the only number that matters

Not followers. Not likes. Track conversion by source: of the people who showed up from each channel, how many took the action you care about (signed up, replied, booked)? Double down on the one or two channels that actually convert and ignore the vanity of the rest.
